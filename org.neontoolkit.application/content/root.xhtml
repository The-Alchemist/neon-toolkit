About the NeOn Technologies Foundation

The NeOn Technologies Foundation is a not-for-profit organization responsible for the management and distribution of the NeOn Toolkit, as well as other technologies originally developed as part of the NeOn Project. The NeOn Technologies Foundation provides a formal vehicle for bringing together those individuals and organizations interested in using or further developing NeOn Technologies.

The goals of the NeOn Technologies Foundation are:

  • To develop and promote the uptake of advanced semantic technologies.
  • To promote high standards in ontology engineering and semantic application design and development through the development and release of robust methods and tools and examples of best practices.
  • To educate students, users and practitioners in semantic technologies through the publication of research outputs and learning resources and the organization of training events.

About the NeOn Toolkit

The NeOn Toolkit is a state-of-the-art, open source multi-platform ontology engineering environment, which provides comprehensive support for the ontology engineering life-cycle. The NeOn Toolkit was originally developed in the context of the EU-funded FP6 NeOn project and it is now distributed and supported by the NeOn Foundation.

About the NeOn Project

The NeOn Project (2006-2010) involved 14 European partners and was co-funded by the European Commission's Sixth Framework Programme under grant number IST-2005-027595. NeOn has developed a variety of methods and technologies to advance the state of the art in the use of ontologies, in particular in the context of semantic web applications. Highlights include methods and tools for managing networks of ontologies, ontology alignment, modularisation, ontology evolution, inconsistency resolution, reuse of ontological and non-ontological resources, navigation and visualisation, to mention just a few. All these technologies are integrated and accessible within the NeOn Toolkit and provide concrete technological support for the NeOn Methodology, a pragmatic handbook-style set of guidelines to guide ontology engineers.

About the NeOn Toolkit Plugins

A large number of plug-ins for the NeOn Toolkit are available, which cover a variety of ontology engineering activities, including Annotation and Documentation, Human-Ontology Interaction, Modularization and Customization, Ontology Debugging, Ontology Dynamics, Ontology Evaluation, Ontology Matching, Ontology Specification, Reasoning and Inference, and Reuse. To install new plugins from within the toolkit, just go to Help > Install new Software.... To update to new versions of already installed plugins go to Help > Check for Updates. The plug-ins for version 2.5.* of the NeOn Toolkit can also be found on the NeOn Toolkit Update Site, http://neon-toolkit.org/plugins/2.5.
